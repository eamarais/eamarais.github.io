Fossil fuel combustion and global public health

Burning of fossil fuels releases large quantities of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere that go on to impact global climate. Here we determine the effect of these on global health. For this, we use GEOS-Chem and an updated risk assessment model developed by epidemiologists at Harvard to calculate premature mortality due to exposure to air pollution from fossil fuels.

People: Eloise Marais (UCL), Karn Vohra (U. Birmingham)
